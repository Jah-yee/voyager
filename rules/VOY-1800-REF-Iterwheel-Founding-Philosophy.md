## What Is It?

The constitutional document for the **Iterwheel** organization. Defines the core
philosophy behind the name and the values that drive every decision in this repo:
iteration, flywheel, compound interest, and *Shu-Ha-Ri* (守破离 — keep the rules,

> *"Every iteration adds a little kinetic energy to the flywheel. The longer it spins, the faster it goes."*

## Origin of the Name

**Iterwheel** = **Iter**(ation) + (Fly)**wheel**

Two seemingly different concepts stacked into one word:

- **Iteration** — stepwise improvement, each round better than the last
- **Flywheel** — cumulative momentum, slow at first, exponential later, never stops

The combined metaphor:

> **Every iteration adds a little kinetic energy to the flywheel. The longer it spins, the faster it goes.**

This is the essence of how knowledge and capability accumulate in the AI era —
the gap looks invisible early on, but compound interest makes the flywheel
spin exponentially faster over time.

## Design Principles

Every decision about naming, workflow, tooling, or bots must satisfy these five
principles:

1. **Narrative consistency** — every name serves the same story: rocket launch
2. **Clear responsibility** — each component does one thing; single responsibility
3. **Extensibility** — when adding a new component, just find the matching rocket-stage word
4. **Sense of ceremony** — a workflow is not just a process; it is the gravity of every launch
5. **Compound spirit** — every Liftoff is not the end, but the start of the next iteration

> Any design that conflicts with these five principles requires a justifying ADR
> before being adopted.
