## What Is It?

Why this repo is named **Voyager**, and what spiritual legacy it inherits.
Voyager is the first long-range rocket built by the Iterwheel factory, and
the best embodiment of the Iterwheel spirit.

---

## Why Voyager

In 1977, NASA launched the twin probes Voyager 1 and Voyager 2.
They were originally meant to study Jupiter and Saturn, but kept flying — and
became the farthest human-made objects in history.

**Nearly 50 years on, they are still operating, still transmitting data back to Earth.**

This is the perfect embodiment of the Iterwheel spirit:

| Iterwheel Spirit | How Voyager Embodies It |
|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Compound** | Almost 50 years of flight — time keeps taking it farther |
| **Flywheel** | **Gravity assists** from planets accelerate it; every flyby spins the wheel faster |
| **Iteration** | Every transmission is a new discovery; every mission extension is an expansion |
| **Spiral ascent** | Jupiter → Saturn → Uranus → Neptune → interstellar space, breakthrough by breakthrough |
| **Never returns** | Once launched, only forward — that is the essence of `release` |

## Spiritual Legacy

Three gifts Voyager left us:

### 1. The Golden Record

Voyager carries a "snapshot of human civilization" addressed to alien life.

> **Every release is the act of packaging our thinking and labor into a container readable by the future.**

Code, docs, commit messages — all are golden records written for future readers.

### 2. Autonomous Operation

Voyager **operates autonomously** at 24 billion kilometers from Earth, running on
preset programs and sparse instructions.

> **This is exactly the spirit of our multi-agent systems: let agents make decisions autonomously at the edge.**

Human-in-the-loop is the ground control center; the agents are the spacecraft itself.

### 3. Gravity Assist

Every borrow-momentum acceleration is the most beautiful physical realization of
flywheel + compound interest.

> Borrow the "gravity" of existing LLMs, tools, and communities to accelerate yourself —
> that is the engineering version of a gravity assist.

## Sister-Mission Naming

Iterwheel can incubate more long-range missions in the future. When the current
project needs a major version bump, or a sibling project is born, expand using
this naming space:

| Codename | Use |
|----------|-----|
| **Voyager** | Current main project |
| **Voyager II** | Honoring the twin-probe heritage — reserved for major version upgrades |
| **Pioneer** | Reserved sister-project codename (Pioneer probes) |
| **New Horizons** | Reserved sister-project codename |
| **Cassini** | Reserved sister-project codename |
| **Hayabusa** | Reserved sister-project codename |

Any new project name must satisfy:

- A real, historical space mission (no invented names)
- The mission's spirit aligns with Iterwheel philosophy
- One sentence explains why this name was chosen (recorded in that project's Mission Charter)
